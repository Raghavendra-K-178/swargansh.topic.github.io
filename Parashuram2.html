Smart City Case Studies with Algorithms

15 Case Studies Applying Algorithms for Smart Cities and Environmental Sustainability

Optimizing Traffic Flow in Smart Cities

SDG Goal: Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure)

Problem: Traffic congestion due to poorly optimized traffic light systems.

Algorithm: Dijkstra’s Algorithm

Solution: Use Dijkstra's algorithm to determine the shortest paths for vehicles and optimize the flow of traffic across the city.

Real-Time Bus Route Optimization

SDG Goal: Goal 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities)

Problem: Public transportation delays and inefficient bus routes.

Algorithm: Prim’s Algorithm

Solution: Apply Prim’s algorithm to optimize bus route scheduling to minimize travel time and maximize coverage.

Smart Water Distribution System Design

SDG Goal: Goal 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation)

Problem: Unequal water distribution leading to shortages in some urban areas.

Algorithm: Kruskal’s Algorithm

Solution: Use Kruskal's algorithm to design an optimal water pipeline network, ensuring equal distribution and minimizing cost.

Minimizing Pollution in Smart Cities

SDG Goal: Goal 13 (Climate Action)

Problem: High pollution levels due to traffic and industrial emissions.

Algorithm: BFS (Breadth-First Search)

Solution: BFS can be used to simulate air pollution propagation and find the most polluted areas, then prioritize interventions.
