Market and Agriculture Case Studies

Market Case Study

Problem 1: Market Price Volatility

  • Algorithm: Bellman-Ford Algorithm
  • Why: Analyzes price trends over time and identifies minimum volatility paths.
  • Approach:
    1. Represent markets as graph nodes and price changes as edge weights.
    2. Use Bellman-Ford to find the minimum cumulative price volatility.
    3. Advise stakeholders on price adjustments.

Problem 2: Supply Chain Inefficiency

  • Algorithm: Dijkstra's Algorithm
  • Why: Optimizes transportation routes by identifying shortest paths.
  • Approach:
    1. Represent supply chains as a weighted graph.
    2. Use Dijkstra’s to find the least-cost or shortest routes.
    3. Implement routing software for real-time transportation decisions.

Agriculture Case Study

Problem 1: Low Crop Yield

  • Algorithm: DFS (Depth-First Search)
  • Why: Analyzes dependencies between soil, crops, and resources.
  • Approach:
    1. Use DFS to explore soil conditions and crop dependencies.
    2. Recommend targeted fertilizer or irrigation strategies.

Problem 2: Water Scarcity for Irrigation

  • Algorithm: Prim’s Algorithm
  • Why: Builds a minimum spanning tree for efficient water distribution.
  • Approach:
    1. Represent farms as nodes and pipelines as weighted edges.
    2. Use Prim’s to minimize water distribution costs.
    3. Ensure equitable water allocation to all farms.
