Smart City Solutions Using Algorithms

1. Industrial Strategies for Smart City Growth

Problem 1: Uneven Industrial Growth

SDG Goal: Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure)

Indicator: 9.2.1 (Manufacturing value added as a proportion of GDP and per capita)

Solution: Use Prim's Algorithm to design a minimum spanning tree that prioritizes connecting underdeveloped regions to industrial hubs.

Problem 2: Resource Allocation Inefficiency

SDG Goal: Goal 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production)

Indicator: 12.2.1 (Material footprint per capita and per GDP)

Solution: Use Dijkstra's Algorithm to optimize resource delivery paths, minimizing time and costs.

2. Institutional Framework for Smart City Governance

Problem 1: Lack of Transparency in Governance

SDG Goal: Goal 16 (Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions)

Indicator: 16.6.1 (Primary government expenditures as a proportion of the original approved budget)

Solution: Use Rabin-Karp Algorithm for efficient pattern matching to detect discrepancies in financial records.

Problem 2: Ineffective Citizen Engagement

SDG Goal: Goal 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities)

Indicator: 11.3.2 (Proportion of cities with a direct participation structure)

Solution: Use Breadth-First Search (BFS) to ensure all communities are included in participatory governance initiatives.
